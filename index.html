Basic Accounting Concepts

Accounting is the **language of business**. It involves recording, classifying, summarizing, and interpreting financial transactions. This page provides a very basic overview of core accounting elements.

The Accounting Equation

The foundation of double-entry accounting is the **Accounting Equation**, which must always remain in balance.

**Assets = Liabilities + Equity**

  • **Assets:** Resources owned by a business (e.g., Cash, Accounts Receivable, Equipment).
  • **Liabilities:** Obligations of a business to outside parties (e.g., Accounts Payable, Loans Payable).
  • **Equity (Owner's/Stockholder's):** The owner's residual claim on the assets after deducting liabilities.
---

Common Account Types

Financial transactions are recorded into different accounts. These accounts are often classified into five main types:

  1. Assets
  2. Liabilities
  3. Equity
  4. Revenue (Increases Equity)
  5. Expenses (Decreases Equity)
---

Example Balance Sheet Elements

The Balance Sheet is a snapshot of a company's assets, liabilities, and equity at a specific point in time. Below are examples of accounts that might appear.

Balance Sheet Accounts
Category Example Accounts
**Assets** Cash, Accounts Receivable, Inventory, Equipment
**Liabilities** Accounts Payable, Salaries Payable, Notes Payable
**Equity** Owner's Capital (or Common Stock, Retained Earnings)
---

Key Accounting Terms

Basic Terminology
Term Description
**Debit** (Dr) The left side of any T-account; increases Assets and Expenses, decreases Liabilities, Equity, and Revenue.
**Credit** (Cr) The right side of any T-account; decreases Assets and Expenses, increases Liabilities, Equity, and Revenue.
**Revenue** Income earned from the normal activities of a business.
**Expense** The cost incurred in the process of generating revenue.

This is a simplified view. Real-world accounting systems involve much more detail and many regulations.
